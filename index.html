Engineering Test Kitchen

Hands on engineering experience for undergrads

Engineering Test Kitchen is a non-profit consulting firm founded and operated by Washington University in St. Louis undergraduate, multi-disciplinary, engineering students. Our mission is to connect engineering undergraduates with real projects where they can learn, make meaningful contributions, and develop relationships with potential future employers.

We provide a team of skilled engineering undergrads under the guidance of an experienced mentor to take on your company’s projects

Each year we pick projects that will provide value to both the company as well as the students that are working on them. The types of project chosen vary from year to year, so if our past projects don’t interest you, apply anyway – we may have a perfect fit for you!

2013-2014

A team worked with Prozess Technologie to do a heat transfer analysis of their product.
Prozess Technologie is a St. Louis-based technology company designing, building, and implementing spectroscopic solutions for process measurement and control in multiple manufacturing sectors.
Website: http://prozesstech.com

2013-2014

A team worked with the Catholic Student Center to do an HVAC analysis of their buildings.
Website: http://www.washucsc.org

2014-2015

A team is working with Tech Manufacturing to refine an existing computer code that models the behavior of equipment performing a manufacturing process. Tech Manufacturing, LLC is a Missouri-based supplier of milled parts to the aerospace industry. Website: http://www.techmanufacturing.com/

A team worked with Project MEGSSS to develop a code-based optimization algorithm. Project MEGSS (Mathematical Education for Gifted Secondary School Students) is a nonprofit organization that offers advanced mathematics classes for talented students. Website: http://megsss.org/
